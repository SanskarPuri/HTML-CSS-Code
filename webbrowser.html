First website: Description of water!

Water is a transparent, tasteless and odorless liquid, essential for life.
The chemical formula of water is, H 2 O.


It composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom. It exists in three states:
  • solid
  • liquid
  • gas
    •
It is a universal solvent, meaning it can dissolve a waide range of substances.
Covering about 71% of Earth's surface, water is crucial for biological processes, acting as a solvent and helping regulate room temprature in living organisms.
⚫Chemical and Physical Properties
  • Composition: Each water molecule consists of two hydrogen atoms covalently bonded to one oxygen atom.

  • States of matter: At standard temperatures, it is a liquid, but it can also exist as a solid (ice), which forms at 0°C (32°F), and a gas (water vapor), which forms at 100°C (212°F).

  • Polarity: Water is a polar molecule, enabling strong intermolecular hydrogen bonding, which contributes to its unique physical properties.

⚫Biological Importance
  • Essential for life: Water is vital for all known life forms, acting as a solvent for biological processes within living organisms.

  • Temprature Regulation: Its high specific heat capacity helps organisms regulate their body temperature by absorbing and releasing large amounts of heat with little change in temperature.

  • Hydration: Organisms, including humans, require regular water intake to replace water lost through processes like sweating and digestion.
⚫Distribution on Earth
  • Global Abundance: Water is the main constituent of the Earth's hydrosphere, covering approximately 71% of the planet's surface in oceans, lakes, and rivers.

  • Natural Cycle: It plays a key role in the water cycle, naturally moving between the clouds and the Earth's surface as rain, rivers, lakes, and oceans.
